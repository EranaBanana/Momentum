The Science of Habit Formation
🧠 How Long It Actually Takes
The popular "21 days" myth comes from a misquoted cosmetic surgery observation. Research from University College London found the real average is 66 days — ranging from 18 to 254 days depending on complexity and the person. Simple habits form faster. Complex ones take longer. There is no universal timeline. The key is showing up consistently, not hitting a magic number.
⚡ The Habit Loop
Every habit runs on a three-part loop: Cue → Routine → Reward. The cue triggers the behavior, the routine is the action, and the reward reinforces it. To build a new habit, attach it to an existing cue (habit stacking) and make the reward immediate and satisfying. Momentum's streak counter and check-off animations are designed to create a small dopamine reward every time you complete a habit.
📉 Why You Miss Days — And What To Do
Missing one day has no measurable impact on habit formation according to the UCL study. The critical rule is never miss twice. One miss is an event. Two misses is the beginning of a new (bad) habit. When you miss, your only job is to make the next day non-negotiable. Lowering the difficulty of the habit after a miss — rather than punishing yourself — is the evidence-based approach.
🎯 Identity-Based Habits
The most durable habits are ones tied to identity rather than outcomes. Instead of "I want to read more," the shift is to "I am someone who reads." Every time you check off a habit, you cast a vote for the identity you are building. Over time those votes accumulate into a self-image that makes the behavior feel automatic rather than effortful. The goal of this app isn't to track your habits — it's to help you become the person those habits belong to.
📏 The Two-Minute Rule
If a new habit takes more than two minutes, it's too big to start with. Scale it down to its smallest possible version. "Run every day" becomes "put on running shoes." "Read every night" becomes "open the book." The point is to establish the pattern of showing up — the duration and intensity come later. Starting small removes resistance and makes the first step frictionless.
How To Use Momentum
Everything you need to know — from first use to long-term data safety.
🚀 Getting Started
1. Add a habit — Click + Habit in the top right (or + New Habit on the Manage tab). Give it a name, pick a color, and most importantly: choose which days it applies to using the day picker.

2. Check off today — On the Today tab you'll see every habit scheduled for today. Tap or click the circle to mark it done. Tap again to undo.

3. Come back tomorrow — That's it. Consistency is the entire game.
🗓️ Setting Up Your Schedule
When adding or editing a habit, use the quick preset buttons (Every day, Weekdays, Mon–Sat, etc.) or manually click individual days to build a custom schedule.

Examples:
• Strength training Mon / Wed / Fri → click M, W, F
• Morning run every day except Sunday → click M T W T F S (leave Sunday off)
• Read 3 times a week → use the 3× preset (Mon, Wed, Fri)

Streaks only count on scheduled days. If Sunday is a rest day, it won't break your streak.
📊 Understanding Your Analytics
Completion Rate — The percentage of scheduled days where you completed the habit. 80% means you hit it 4 out of every 5 scheduled days.

Streaks — Consecutive scheduled days completed. A streak of 10 on a Mon–Fri habit means you've been perfect for 2 full weeks of working days.

Best Days of Week — Shows your average completion % for each day, calculated across the last 90 days. Use this to spot your strong and weak days.

Perfect Days — Days where every single scheduled habit was completed. This is your most demanding metric.

Heatmap — One cell per day for the past year. Darker = more habits completed. Hover any cell to see the exact count.
💾 How to Back Up Your Data
Your data lives in your browser's local storage — which means it's private and stays on your device, but it can be lost if you clear your browser data. Back up regularly.

To export a backup:
1. Go to the Advanced tab
2. Click Export JSON
3. Save the file somewhere safe — your Downloads folder, Google Drive, iCloud, Dropbox, a USB drive, or email it to yourself

Recommended backup frequency:
• Casual use → once a month
• Active daily use → once a week
• Long streak you can't afford to lose → after every milestone
📲 Moving to a New Device or Browser
Momentum data does not automatically sync between devices — it's stored locally in whichever browser you use it in. To move your data:

Step 1: On your current device, go to Advanced → Export JSON and save the file.
Step 2: Copy the HTML file and the JSON backup to your new device (email, cloud drive, USB).
Step 3: Open the HTML file in your new browser.
Step 4: Go to Advanced → Import JSON and select your backup file.

Your complete history, habits, streaks, and settings will be fully restored.
⚠️ What Will Delete Your Data
Your data is stored in browser local storage. The following actions will permanently erase it if you haven't exported a backup:

• Clearing browser cache or site data (Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data)
• Using private/incognito mode — local storage does not persist after the window closes
• Uninstalling or reinstalling the browser
• Clicking Reset All Data in Settings
• Using a different browser — Chrome and Safari do not share local storage with each other

Data is NOT lost by: closing the tab, restarting your computer, normal browser updates, or closing and reopening the HTML file normally.

The safest habit is exporting a JSON backup once a week and keeping it somewhere you won't lose it.
🏆 Best Practices
Keep habits specific and small to start. "Drink water" is better than "be healthier." You can always add more habits later.

Use the Why field. When you add a habit, fill in your reason. On hard days when you open the app, seeing your own words is more motivating than any default message.

Don't track more than you can realistically do. 5 habits you actually do beats 15 habits you half-do.

Check the History tab after a rough week. Seeing both done and missed days gives you an honest picture — and often shows you did better than you remembered.

Export monthly. Set a reminder. Takes 10 seconds. Saves years of data.

🔗 Developer API
Access your data programmatically from the browser console. Completely optional — the app works perfectly without this.
// Read all data const data = JSON.parse(localStorage.getItem('momentum_data')); console.log(data.habits, data.logs); // Log a habit completion manually const today = new Date().toISOString().split('T')[0]; data.logs.push({ habitId: 'YOUR_HABIT_ID', date: today, ts: Date.now() }); localStorage.setItem('momentum_data', JSON.stringify(data)); location.reload(); // Data shape: // habits: [{ id, name, category, days:[0-6], color, why, created }] // logs: [{ habitId, date:'YYYY-MM-DD', ts }]
